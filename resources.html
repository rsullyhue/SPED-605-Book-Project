AAC Resources and Support

This page compiles helpful resources for families navigating AAC. Links are organized by category to help you find what you need.

AAC Organizations and Information

ASHA (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association)

Professional organization for SLPs with extensive AAC information for families.

www.asha.org/public/speech/disorders/aac

AAC Institute

Provides training, resources, and information about AAC research and implementation.

www.aacinstitute.org

PrAACtical AAC

Blog and resource site with practical AAC implementation strategies for families and professionals.

www.praacticalaac.org

AssistiveWare

AAC app company with excellent free resources, including the Core Word Classroom and Learn AAC.

www.assistiveware.com/learn-aac

Parent Support and Communities

AAC and Autism Facebook Group

Large, active community of parents and professionals supporting AAC use for individuals with autism.

Search "AAC and Autism" on Facebook

The Speak for Yourself AAC Community

Support group for families using Speak for Yourself app, but welcomes all AAC users.

Search "Speak for Yourself" on Facebook

Communication Community (by Tobii Dynavox)

Online community for AAC users, families, and professionals to share tips and support.

www.tobiidynavox.com/community

Funding and Grants

AAC Funding Help

Comprehensive database of funding sources organized by state.

www.aacfundinghelp.com

United Healthcare Children's Foundation

Provides grants up to $5,000 for medical equipment not covered by insurance.

www.uhccf.org

Variety - The Children's Charity

Provides adaptive equipment including communication devices to children in need.

www.usvariety.org

The Luka Autism Foundation

Provides iPads with AAC apps to children with autism.

www.lukafoundation.org

Training and Education

Project Core

Free online modules teaching core word instruction for students with complex communication needs.

www.project-core.com

QIAT (Quality Indicators for Assistive Technology)

Resources and indicators for quality AT services, including AAC.

www.qiat.org

Yale AAC Research Group

Research-based resources on AAC implementation, particularly for children with autism.

Search "Yale AAC Research" for current resources

Visual displays of research evidence for various communication interventions including AAC.

Find an AAC Specialist

ASHA ProFind

Search for certified SLPs with AAC expertise in your area.

www.asha.org/profind

ATIA (Assistive Technology Industry Association)

Professional organization for AT specialists, including AAC consultants.

www.atia.org

Device Trial Programs

Many AAC companies offer trial or loan programs. Contact companies directly to ask about:

  • Length of trial period (typically 30-60 days)
  • Cost (some are free, others require deposit)
  • Eligibility requirements
  • Technical support during trial
  • Whether you work through your SLP or directly with the company

Remember

This is not an exhaustive list, and new resources emerge regularly. Join parent support groups and connect with your local AAC community to discover additional resources in your area.
